Linear Motion

Linear motion, also called rectilinear motion, is one-dimensional motion along a straight line, and can therefore be described mathematically using only one spatial dimension.

Key Takeaways:

  • Distance and displacement are different measures of motion
  • Speed is the rate of change of position
  • Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity
  • Motion can be described using equations of motion

Conservative of Energy

The law of conservation of energy states that the total energy of an isolated system remains constant; it is said to be conserved over time.

Key Takeaways:

  • Energy cannot be created or destroyed
  • Energy can be converted between different forms
  • Total energy remains constant in an isolated system
  • Work done equals change in energy
